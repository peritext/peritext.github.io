Introduction

This document presents the different components of the Peritext project. You want to directly explore what does the project allows in terms of writing, design and publishing ? test the web version of the Ovide editor or visit examples of realizations.
Peritext aims at equipping sensible and polymorphic scholarly writing and publishing practices through the making and experimentation of free and open digital tools. It articulates:
  • a shared data model
  • a family of javascript code modules
  • a series of writing and publishing software accessible on the web
  • diverse experiments and iterations through pedagogical sequences, collective work sessions and specific editorial projects
Peritext allows researchers and other writers:
  • to set up a corpus of writings and annotations for a set of research materials (references, tables, videos, images, ...)
  • to write in the same space drafts and other research notes, and finalized chapters ready for publication
  • to publish different aspects of a work in progress (annotated bibliographies, case studies, reports, essays) in the form of high quality web and print documents
  • to produce working documents dedicated to publishing and design professionals, without detracting from their intentions in terms of contents staging
Peritext allows publishers and editorial designers:
  • to have a working interface with authors in the context of editorial projects that call for a significant amount of non-textual resources
  • to have a means of publishing for both print and the web, while having total control over the editorial quality of each of the documents produced
  • to produce prototypes and other editorial "semi-finished" products that can be refined with other more advanced tools
Peritext allows developpers and interface designers:
  • to have a data model for managing editorial content that is both complete and expressible in JSON format, allowing the implementation of lightweight writing/editing systems (without database or even server)
  • to have a central schema and a set of interoperable javascript modules to build editorial software that can call up a diversity of writing materials
  • to have a modular code base for the construction of a scholarly content editor

Write as close as possible to the research materials

Peritext is designed to allow various writing strategies anchored in a very close relationship to the materials (data, sources, documents, ...) collected during a research activity. In a Peritext "production", writings can be attached as much to a note as to images, videos, data files, places, dates, persons, etc. Any element can be a starting point for writing and developing analysis.
Peritext thus makes it possible to structure the writing of research content in a traditional way (in chapters and sub-chapters) but also to use the diverse "materials" of a research as a structure for argumentation and reflection. It is thus possible to write starting from a bibliographical reference to record reading notes, to comment/analyse a specific image, to produce the detailed exploration of a data file in the manner of a notebook, etc.
The project also invites scholarly authors to intertwine text and other research material intimately by allowing them to specify numerous parameters for the integration of research material into texts (e.g., start and end times for a video, visualization parameters for a data table, etc.).
It thus allows researchers to compare data files via their visualization, to call up extracts from interviews, to stage the analysis of videos and other images, and so on.

Publishing for the multitude

Peritext is also a publishing technology. It allows, from a corpus of writings made of notes and other interconnected materials, to generate a multitude of editions for printing and display on screen. These editions are ready for publication, but can also be completely modified and re-edited using other tools.
Each edition of a Peritext "production" can be designed according to the objectives, audiences or stage of research (research notebook, progress report, conclusive publication, etc.). Each edition has a specific composition, but can also be entirely restyled and be the subject of specific graphic and interactive design work.
From a given corpus of writings, Peritext thus makes it possible to publish intermediate research works (working papers, elements of research notebooks, publications backing seminar-type events, etc.), the publication of companions or attachments to classic scientific publications, or contexts outside the academic field, such as those of journalism, amateur scholarly publication or higher education (dissertations, theses, etc.).
